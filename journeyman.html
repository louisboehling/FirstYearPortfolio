Create a product or service that challenges the status quo.

Journeyman is a peer-to-peer education service that connects people who are interested in experiential learning.

Sign Up

Users enter basic information and indicate their insterests with pre-loaded categories. Users also have the option to login with Facebook or Google, which will pull likes, interests, and necessary metadata to populate a suggestions list.

Search

Search for classes by category, dates, number of students, cost, or location. Scroll through the results generated by user search inputs. View the course titles, one line descriptions, teacher grades, and costs, all from the results page.

Explore Courses

Course profiles provide a class overview where students learn about their teacher and what they will learn. A floating book now button displays the name of the course and the total price for the specified duration.

Share What You Know

Teachers are carefully vetted and must meet the Journeyman standards in order to become and remain educators. Applications are reviewed by the Journeyman team. If a course is approved, the class page is created using the content provided in the application.

Ideation Process

Our team recognized that most Americans who travel choose to do so in hopes of relaxing, exploring, and/or meeting new people to understand other lifestyles. However, people rarely travel to learn new skills.


Initial Feedback

We were surprised after our first round of feedback from users.

“I don’t know how I’d feel about staying with someone I just met while they teach me a skill. I use AirBnB to save money ... not to spend all of my time with the host. But I like the idea of hands-on learning!” - Jeremy


Removing our Blinders

At first, this feedback was difficult to accept. Our team had crafted the idea from the fabric of personal experience. We knew first-hand the value of getting to know a host while learning new cultural perspectives. However, we quickly recognized that most Americans have not had these experiences, and our idea may not appeal to more introverted or hesitant travelers.


Discovery 2.0

Thus, we shifted our focus to the education system, while still encouraging the exploration of new lifestyles, but through the lens of education. We wanted to build a system that would be mutually beneficial for both students and teachers.

We define experiential learning as a process through which students develop knowledge, skills, and values from direct experiences outside a traditional academic setting.

We found that our best ideas came from our own personal experiences. We wanted to map out a way to share our own experiences out in the world with people at home.

Strategy

By prioritizing education above travel, we can appeal to students who want to learn without traveling, while also appealing to students who want to learn while traveling.


Competitive Analysis

Online platforms such as YouTube, Lynda.com, and Skillshare are affordable options for people who want to learn new skills, but these companies are limited in what they can teach and fail to deliver the in person learning experience that students value.

The analysis helped us find the need in the market for an accessible lifelong learning program that places value on a strong student-teacher relationships.

Ideation Process

Desktop vs Mobile?

Initially our team considered developing a mobile app, however our research showed that an overwhelming majority of users ages 18-36 still prefer to book travel on a desktop interface. Thus, we shifted our focus from a mobile app to a responsive desktop design.


Our objective was to design an accessible lifelong learning program that places value on a strong student-teacher relationships.

Low-fidelity wireframes made in Sketch helped us map out user flows and make critical art direction choices during the ideation phase.

Style Guidelines

The design of the website was meant to convey trust and authenticity to the user. The mixture of handwritten text with serif and sans serif fonts was purposeful in that the pairing of those fonts evokes a sense of beginning and learning, yet also a sense of value and structure as well. In that same essence, the colors we chose for the website are more subdued, scholarly, and welcoming. The handdrawn elements are a way to bring in a sense of authenticity and a more educational look to the overall design of the site.

Takeaway

Team and My Role

Our team was originally assigned different roles (strategy, UI/UX, and art direction) however we really found that we all started to take on each of these roles in our own way. I was originally assigned UI/UX, however the art director and I fleshed out a vision we had so throughly that it was not apparent where her role stopped and mine began. She also took on UI/UX work.

This project was a testament to the idea that “stay in your lane mentality” is not always the best case in this kind of creative work. While we were respectful of each other’s roles, we found that we arrived at the best work and most thorough product when our inputs and ideas crossed those boundaries.

Over all, my role in the project was predominately in UI/UX work, art direction, and concept development.
