28 years in short

I grew up outside of Richmond, Virginia in a family where we were always encouraged to play outside and be creative.

After graduating from the University of Mary Washington with a B.A. in Spanish Language and Literature (concentration in Latin American studies) and a minor in Biochemistry I got my first job as a graphic designer.

Two years later, I moved from Virginia to Hoi An, Vietnam. It was one of the most transformative experiences of my life. For two years I worked as a graphic designer and web developer for Hoi An Events, Hoi An Now Magazine and freelancing.

In 2018 I was accepted to the VCU Brandcenter where I am currently pursuing my Masters in Business and Branding in the Experience Design track.

See all the details on my résumé (which was featured on Bestfolios).


Muses, etc.

My interests inspire my best work. Travel. Literature. Cooking. Language. History. Film. Health. Family. These are all things that get me in the mood to create.

I love language and over the past ten years have learned to speak French and Spanish. I am currently learning German and Mandarin with the language learning app DuoLingo.

Story inspires me. From film to writing to myth and beyond I have always been an appreciator of intelligent storytelling paired with intriguing visuals. Some of my favorite storytellers are Lars Von Trier, J.R.R. Tolkien, Sofia Coppola, and Hayao Miyazaki.

On any given day you could find me reading, running on the trails by the river, or having an espresso at a café in the sun.

And cooking. I really love to cook. I even wrote a book about baking sourdough bread.

About this site

This site was hand-coded by me using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Node.js, and Bootstrap.

I went through many design iterations and landed on this simple, clean, straightforward design that would put my work first and the design of the page second.

The small animations were created using JavaScript and the NPM plugin Animate.css — a simple CSS animation kit.

The fonts used are two of my favorites: Work Sans for the body copy and Archivo Black for the bold headlines.

Coding has always been one of my favorite ways to relax and to create without pen and paper.
