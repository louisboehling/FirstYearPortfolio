Uniqlove by Uniqlo
Product Launch

Problem

How can people share a physical connection from a distance?


Opportunity

Although we are more connected than ever, recent global surveys show trends towards isolation and long-distance relationships.

Product Design
Strategy
UI/UX Design

The Uniqlo Uniqlove heart allows people to share tactile connection through heat over long distances.

Touch your heart to send your partner some love

The heart can be placed in the shirt pocket or pants back pocket. It is paired with your phone through Bluetooth and messages are sent using a data connection such as 4G.

To activate one partner presses their heart device for two seconds until they feel a small vibration to confirm that they sent a warm sensation to the other paired heart. In this iteration of the product the heart can only be paired to one other heart.

In order to manage the device all sent and received love can be seen via the app. Pairing and battery levels can also be monitored in the app.

Our team identified a wide variety of users

Initially we envisioned young lovers would use this product as a fun interface to share love and also as an Instagramable moment.

Through speaking to potential users we also identified several other groups: the family with a deployed loved one, parents and children, grandparents, and lovers who are looking to put some zest back into their relationship.

Valentine's Day product launch

The Uniqlo Uniqlove heart is a a product meant for limited release starting on Valentine’s Day, February 14, 2020. It will be available online and in the store. There will be a pop-up store visual experience in large cities such as New York, Paris, and Tokyo.

Growth opportunities for the Uniqlove heart

In the future our team saw many opportunities for the Uniqlo heart. We identified a potential partnership with the RED organization. Future iterations of the product could innovate further with more technology such as smell, multiple-connections, and vibrations. The product could also be diversified for more user-specific needs. We identified several avenues such as the kinky couple, a pack for families, best friends, and active couples who like sports.

Takeaway

The team and my role

I worked with my art director friends Mila and Jesse on this project. We crafted this product from our own experiences all having been in long-distance relationships and knowing how nice it is to receive a small token of love during the day, wether it be a text saying “I love you” or something more.

My role in this project was to flesh out the interworking of the heart: how it would connect to 4G via Bluetooth, and what tech would be used to do this. I also built the app and screens. We all worked together to develop the product idea.
