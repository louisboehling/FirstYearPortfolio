Weave The Wild Hangtags

Weave the Wild, a local textile vendor in Hoi An, Vietnam that sources its fabrics from the women of the Red Dzao H’mong hill tribe in Sapa, Vietnam asked me to help them create some new hangtags for a new line of pillows in their shop.

Cut off from the rest of the country, the hill tribe people have struggled to find their identity in a rapidly modernizing Vietnam. Despite Sapa’s popularity with tourists, poverty is still a reality for many of the local people who lack basic eduction and access to heath care. A percentage of my client’s profits are returned to the women of the Red Dzao on her biannual trips to Sapa.

Using the hill tribe dying process as inspiration, together we came up with the idea of a hangtag that looked as if it had been dipped in batik — the indigo dye used to make the textiles. We dipped pieces of card stock into the ink to create different patterns. I studied how the ink flowed on the paper, the patterns made by the bubbles, and the small arrays of color from deep violets to light blues. I made a composite of the dip-dyed paper in Photoshop. We tweaked the design and placed a handwritten logo above.
