Weave The Wild Hangtags


Weave the Wild, a local textile vendor in Hoi An, Vietnam that sources its fabrics from the women of the Red Dzao H’mong hill tribe in Sapa, Vietnam asked me to help them create some new hangtags for a new line of pillows in their shop. Using the hill tribe dying process as inspiration, together we came up with the idea of a hangtag that looked as if it had been dipped in batik — the indigo dye used to make the textiles. We dipped pieces of card stock into the ink to create different patterns and I reproduced them digitally for the final product.
