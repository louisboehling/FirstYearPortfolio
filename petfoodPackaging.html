Reimagining
Pet Food Packaging

Ask

Create a new use for pet food packaging to extend its life and develop a circular model for pet owners to receive their pet food.


Opportunity

The current life cycle of a polypropylene pet food bag ends in a landfill almost 100% of the time. Polypropylene is one of the least recycled post-consumer plastics at a rate below 1% for post-consumer recovery.

Product Design
Strategy
UI/UX Design

What kind of system could responsibly recycle polypropylene pet food bags and keep them out of landfills for good?

A Zero-Waste Solution

Our team designed a new system for Petco’s WholeHearted food by recycling existing pet food bags into a “Let’s Eat” product line and eliminating the need for future bags by creating a zero- waste way of distributing WholeHearted food.

Our strategy is to reimagine pet food packaging to make the pet owner happier and the environment healthier.

Pet Food Dispensing System

In the space currently occupied by Petco’s WholeHearted Line of bagged food a series of gravity dispensers would be placed on the wall. Food is dispensed using the application interface on the screen that is attached to the tube into a container.

Petco will offer a line of rolling containers also made of polypropylene. These containers are designed with a lip on the front that will allow them to clip into the dispensing tubes. The food is dispensed straight into the container so customers never have to interface with the food.

A Simple Interface

Food is dispensed using the application interface on the screen that is attached to the tube. Much like the physical experience of picking up a bag of food, users can learn more about the food contents and select different amounts by volume.

"Let's Eat" by WholeHearted

We also wanted to design a new way to reuse polypropylene packaging to delay the landfill portion of the process. Petco currently has a dog food brand called WholeHearted that uses natural ingredients. We decided this line would be best for our new product innovation “Let’s Eat.” “Let’s Eat” offers a variety of customizable dog food bowls and storage containers.

Survey and Research

It all started with a trip to pet stores. We went to Dogma, Petco, and Dog Krazy in Richmond, Virginia, and asked employees about the current pet food process. What did consumers like in pet food? Did they even notice the packaging? Did they ask about recycling? We let the conversation flow.


We gathered our notes and built a survey. We collected data from 55 pet owners and found that they don’t like pet food packaging because it’s not resealable, difficult to store, hard to carry, and they don’t even know if it’s recyclable or not.


After collecting pain points on the packaging, we conducted research on polypropylene and the pet food industry to try to determine what solutions currewntly existed. Why do pet food companies use this material and how could this packaging become something else?

Ideation

Our first round of brainstorming included making pet food packaging into building materials for developing countries or for dog parks. But after thinking through this, we decided the solution should be something more easily incorporated into the existing cycle.


We needed to narrow down our thinking for solutions. We decided to focus on Petco because it’s the second top pet retailer in the USA and it has an existing rewards program that can be used to incentivize consumers to be a part of our solutions. Petco is also open to innovation. It is already experimenting with new ways to dispense kitty litter and treats.


Our first testable solution went BIG! It was all about a entirely new and complicated pressurized distribution system built into a room at Petco that can serve up any pet food on demand via a user interface. Pet owners would use a bin made out of polypropylene to use the system.


We quickly realized this was too expensive and complicated. How would Petco pay for this? Why would pet food brands want to be a part of this? We then narrowed our thinking down to Petco’s own WholeHearted food line. This let us experiment more with Petco’s rewards program and existing pet food systems.

Early sketches of our product made using Procreate and Apple Pencil.

Testing

Though our testing we found that people really gravitated towards customization and the new store experience with their pets. Testing also urged us to address the issue of the moment a pet owner inevitably forgets their bin, so we developed a new in-store tote system.


We tested our solution with users by walking them through the user journey. We described the experience to them and then asked them to note any pain points that they saw in the system.

The original user journey is in red, our new user journey is in blue. The observed deltas in the search process, transportation, and transfer show that our solution makes the pet food purchasing process a positive experience that is also eco-friendly.
